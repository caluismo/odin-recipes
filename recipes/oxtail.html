Oxtail Recipe

Description

Tender, fall-off-the-bone oxtail slow-cooked in a rich, savory broth with herbs, spices, and vegetables. This hearty stew is full of deep, comforting flavors and is perfect for cozy dinners. Traditionally served with rice and peas or mashed potatoes, the dish combines melt-in-your-mouth beef with a flavorful sauce that’s perfect for soaking up with bread. It’s a soulful, satisfying meal that brings warmth and comfort to the table.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs oxtail pieces
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 potatoes, cubed
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme (or 1 tsp dried)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp allspice (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a large pot or Dutch oven. Season oxtail with salt and pepper, then sear on all sides until browned. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, sauté onion, garlic, and bell pepper until softened.
  3. Stir in tomato paste and cook for 1 minute. Add diced tomatoes and mix well.
  4. Return oxtail to the pot. Pour in beef broth, add thyme, bay leaves, and allspice. Bring to a boil.
  5. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 2–3 hours, until oxtail is tender and meat nearly falls off the bone.
  6. Add carrots and potatoes. Simmer another 30–40 minutes, until vegetables are tender.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. Garnish with parsley and serve hot with rice, peas, or bread.
